Project overview

VR Chaotic Kitchen is a short round based experience that borrows structure from cooking competition shows. At the start of each round a giant screen reveals a theme such as Pasta Night, Breakfast Battle, or Comfort Food. From that moment, both players sprint into a shared low poly pantry, grab ingredients they think fit the theme, and carry them back to their stations before the doors close.

During the cooking phase, players chop, cook, and assemble dishes while reacting to surprise chaos events that temporarily change how the kitchen works. When time expires, plates slide onto a spotlighted judging counter where our AI host reviews the choices, assigns scores, and explains which dish most matched the prompt.

Gameplay and learning goals

Round structure

  1. Theme reveal. The studio monitor reveals the round theme with a quick host voice line and visual card.
  2. Pantry sprint. Both players rush into the shared pantry and collect raw ingredients before the timer or doors lock in.
  3. Cooking phase. Players chop, cook, and assemble dishes on their stations while chaos events briefly alter the kitchen.
  4. Plating and garnish. In the final seconds players plate food, add finishing touches, and place their dishes on the judging counter.
  5. Judging ceremony. The AI host evaluates theme fit, ingredient synergy, and timing, then announces scores and names the winner.

Skills the experience practices

  • Communication under time pressure when calling out ingredients, chaos events, and timers.
  • Shared resource management when the pantry contains only one copy of key ingredients.
  • Flexible planning when chaos events force mid round strategy changes instead of full resets.
  • Situational awareness in VR while tracking the other player, the stove state, and environmental cues.
System
Shared low poly pantry

Shelves and tables hold color coded ingredients for fruits, vegetables, proteins, sauces, and specialty items. The layout is readable at a glance so players can make fast decisions instead of hunting for tiny props.

Mechanic
Controlled chaos events

A lightweight chaos manager triggers short events during the cooking phase that are playful but legible.

  • Teleporting ingredients between stations.
  • Temporary gravity changes that cause items to float or feel heavy.
  • Stove quirks that require players to move pans or reset burners.
Feedback
AI host judging

The AI host evaluates each round using simple rule based scores for theme match, ingredient variety, and completion. Lines are written to sound like a friendly competition show host so players understand why they won or lost.

Audience
Where this fits

VR Chaotic Kitchen is tuned for lab showcases, outreach events, and short classroom labs where instructors want students to reflect on communication, improvisation, and handling chaotic constraints in collaborative work.
