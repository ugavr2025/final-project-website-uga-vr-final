Project Overview
Designed to teach soft skills ‚Äì not just entertain

VR Chaotic Kitchen is a two player VR cooking show set in a stylized studio kitchen. Each round has a theme, a shared pantry, a strict time limit, and a series of short chaos events that disrupt normal cooking flow. Both players pull ingredients from the same pantry, rush back to their stations, and assemble a dish that fits the round theme such as Pasta Night or Comfort Food.

At the end of the timer, dishes move to a central judging counter where an AI host scores each plate based on theme fit, doneness, plating, and time. The goal is to make players feel like contestants on a cooking competition show while secretly training skills like shared resource negotiation, situational awareness, and communication under pressure.

Collaborative competition Shared pantry and limited resources Chaos event system AI judging with breakdown Short, high energy rounds

üéØ Core experience goals

  • Feel like you are on a live cooking competition show.
  • Encourage teamwork, friendly rivalry, and constant communication.
  • Make grabbing, chopping, cooking, and plating feel tactile and satisfying.
  • Use chaos events to force flexible strategies and quick recovery.
  • Keep visuals low poly and readable so the focus stays on interaction.
Gameplay and Features
From theme reveal to big screen winner

Round based gameplay loop

  1. Theme reveal ‚Äì a large studio screen announces the round theme such as Pasta or Breakfast, with a short host line.
  2. Pantry rush ‚Äì a countdown starts and both players sprint to the shared pantry to grab ingredients before the other player does.
  3. Cooking phase ‚Äì players chop, cook, mix, and assemble a dish at their own station while chaos events fire in the background.
  4. Plating and presentation ‚Äì in the final seconds, players move their plates to the judging counter and add garnish for style points.
  5. Judging phase ‚Äì the AI judge inspects both plates, scores them, and announces the winner on the big screen. Optionally a human judge can add a style bonus.
  6. Next round ‚Äì a new theme, higher chaos, and tougher time constraints keep the session fresh.
Shared pantry
Mechanic

Panic at the pantry door

Both players draw from the same pantry shelves, which turns early seconds of each round into a light collision of priorities. Do you grab safe essentials or rush for rare ingredients that could push your dish over the top.

  • Simple physical grabbing and placing of ingredient prefabs.
  • Theme rules encourage varied but understandable recipes.
  • Short timers keep decisions fast rather than deeply planned.
Chaos manager
System

Controlled chaos events

A Chaos Manager triggers short disruptions during the cooking phase. Each event is funny but readable, and the kitchen always returns to normal.

  • Teleporting ingredients between stations.
  • Levitating or spinning items that are harder to grab.
  • Giant utensils or rubber knife mode that briefly affect control.
  • Stove flare ups that force players to move pans.
AI host
Feedback

Judging and scoring breakdown

At the end of each round, an AI judge evaluates each plate using simple template rules tied to the theme.

  • Theme fit ‚Äì required ingredient types present or missing.
  • Doneness ‚Äì cooked, raw, or burnt states from the cooking system.
  • Plating ‚Äì number of items on the plate and garnish choices.
  • Time bonus ‚Äì extra points for finishing before the buzzer.
Collaboration
Multiplayer

Two player shared kitchen

Both players inhabit one continuous studio set with spatial audio and full body movement. They must dodge each other around counters, negotiate ingredient picks, and react to the same chaos events.

  • Shared world state for ingredients, plates, timers, and chaos.
  • Supports friendly rivalry or future cooperative modes.
Skills and Learning Goals
Practice real world collaboration through play

üß† What the experience teaches

  • Communication under time pressure ‚Äì players need to call out ingredient choices, timers, and chaos events while still working their own station.
  • Shared resource management ‚Äì the pantry is finite, so players must negotiate or adapt when someone else grabs a key ingredient first.
  • Flexible planning ‚Äì chaos events change the environment in the middle of a recipe, which pushes players to adjust plans instead of restarting.
  • Situational awareness ‚Äì players track cook times, kitchen layout, and the other player while moving in VR.

üè´ Target audiences

While the experience is playful, it is designed as a practice tool rather than pure entertainment.

Student workshop activity Team icebreaker for labs Soft skills training XR demo for showcases

Technical Implementation
Under the hood for developers and judges

üõ† Tools and systems

  • Unity with XR support targeting Meta Quest 3.
  • Low poly kitchen and food assets from curated packs for readability.
  • Custom Chaos Manager that schedules events and resets state.
  • Theme and recipe templates for flexible judging rather than fixed recipes.
  • Multiplayer synchronization of ingredients, timers, plates, and scores.
  • Simple AI judging script that produces a breakdown per category.

üìÜ Implementation phases

Phase 1 ‚Äì Core kitchen and cooking
Layout of shared pantry and stations, ingredient states, chopping and stove interactions, timer and theme UI.
Phase 2 ‚Äì Rounds and judging
Theme templates, judging counter, AI scoring breakdown, and scoreboard at the end of each round.
Phase 3 ‚Äì Chaos events
Chaos Manager with initial events and banner plus audio cues for each disruption.
Phase 4 ‚Äì Multiplayer and polish
Two synchronized player rigs, shared state, timing and scoring tuning, lighting, and final UI adjustments for the public demo.
